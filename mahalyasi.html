The 4-Pillar Growth Architecture

📍

Pillar 1 — Search Presence (GMB + Website)

Own local search before spending on ads — this is the foundation

When someone in Adoor searches "ayurveda near me" or "postnatal care Adoor," Mahalyasi must appear. Right now, competitors with weak services outrank better clinics simply because they're on Google. This is fixable in 30 days.

  • Google My Business: Full optimization — categories, photos, services, Q&A, weekly posts, review engine. Free real estate most clinics waste.
  • 4-Page Website (₹28k): Home, Services, Postnatal Care, Beauty Clinic, Contact + Appointment. WhatsApp CTA on every page. Mobile-first. Fast-loading.
  • JustDial + Sulekha + Practo listings: Free local authority signals. Drives calls directly.
  • SAG·EO Foundation: FAQ content on website answers real patient questions — postnatal care safety, beauty clinic process, package inclusions. Ranks over time, works forever.
📣

Pillar 2 — Brand Awareness (Social + Content)

Build recognizability in the 10km radius before scaling

People don't book Ayurveda from a single ad. They see the brand multiple times — a Reel, a GMB photo, a friend mentioning it, a story. We build that frequency at low cost through content.

  • Instagram Reels: 2/week. Authentic clinic footage — not AI videos. Real therapists, real treatments, real space. This is the #1 driver of organic reach.
  • Facebook Posts: 2/week. Educational content (postnatal care tips, what is Shirodhara) + service features.
  • WhatsApp Broadcast: 3x/month to growing patient list. Offers, health tips, monthly specials — never spam.
  • Content from limited shoots: 2 structured shoot days/month → 30+ days of content. See Shoot Plan tab for breakdown.
🎯

Pillar 3 — Lead Generation (Meta Ads)

Paid traffic for the services with highest conversion potential

With ₹10k ad spend, we run 2 targeted campaigns at a 10–15km radius. Lead gen form ads (no website needed in Month 1). Audience: women 25–45, Pathanamthitta + surrounding districts.

  • Campaign 1 — ₹1,999 Rejuvenation Package: Already proven to sell. Lowest friction entry point. Run as lead gen + WhatsApp click.
  • Campaign 2 — Postnatal Care / Beauty Clinic: Rotate monthly based on which has more capacity. Postnatal: women 24–36. Beauty: women 20–40.
  • Re-targeting (Month 2+): Website visitors and video viewers get a follow-up offer ad. Cost per lead drops by 40–60%.
  • Phase 2 — Custom Audience from Jayakrishnan's lead DB: Upload phone numbers from other business contacts (with permission) → Meta Custom Audience → show Mahalyasi ads to warm prospects.
🔁

Pillar 4 — Retention & Referral Engine

Existing patients are the cheapest lead source — activate them

One patient who refers 2 friends is worth more than 5 cold ad leads. We build a simple, no-tech referral loop into the clinic's daily workflow.

  • Post-treatment review ask: Every patient leaving → staff shows QR code for Google review. Target 20 reviews by end of Month 1.
  • WhatsApp referral: "Share this message with a friend and both get a free add-on session on your next visit." No app needed.
  • Clinic-to-hospital pipeline: Approach 2–3 maternity hospitals/gynecology clinics in 10km radius. Offer them postnatal care brochures to keep at reception. Revenue share or simple referral agreement.
  • Seasonal campaigns: Karkidaka (monsoon) = panchakarma push. Post-onam = beauty clinic. Festival = gift voucher campaign.

Content Production Strategy

Maximum Content from Minimum Shoots

2 structured shoot days per month generates 30+ days of content. Here's exactly how we plan it — and what we extract from each visit.

The Principle: One well-planned shoot day at the clinic can produce 4–6 Reels, 8–10 static posts, 15+ Stories, and 3–4 ad creatives — if we plan it right. We brief the shots in advance, shoot systematically, and batch-edit everything. The cameraman (from their end or a local freelancer we brief) needs a clear shot list, not creative freedom.
Cameraman Strategy: We recommend Mahalyasi coordinate a local freelance videographer from Adoor/Pathanamthitta (₹1,500–2,500/day rate). We at FlumenX provide the full shot list, brief, and direction document. We review footage and edit. Client doesn't need to manage the creative — just coordinate the shoot day (patients available, treatments happening, space clean). One day/month is enough to start. We scale to 2 days if needed.

Risk Register

Risks We See — And How We Handle Them

Identified before they become problems. Mitigated intelligently.

HIGH

2-Room Capacity Ceiling

If campaigns succeed fast, appointment demand could exceed capacity before Month 2. Overbooking kills patient experience and generates negative reviews — undoing all marketing work. Mitigation: Track room utilization weekly. If consistently above 75%, immediately flag to Mr. Jayakrishnan for capacity conversation. Never sacrifice experience for volume. We reduce ad spend before we overbook.

HIGH

₹10k/Month Ad Budget — Tight for Lead Gen

₹10k across 10–15km radius can deliver 18–30 leads/month if creatives perform. If shoot assets aren't ready Week 2, ads go live with inferior creatives and CPL suffers. Mitigation: Shoot Day 1 must happen in Week 2. We prepare at least 1 ad creative from phone photos as backup for Day 1 if shoot is delayed.

MED

Shoot Coordination Dependency

Content quality depends on a clinic-side shoot happening. If the freelance videographer or patient timing doesn't align, we lose 2 weeks of feed content. Mitigation: We prepare a full "no-shoot content pack" — text-on-background posts, herb/product flat lays the staff can shoot on phone using our brief. Enough for 10 posts without a cameraman.

MED

Patient Consent for Beauty Before/After Content

Beauty clinic result photos are the highest-performing content type but require explicit consent. Not collecting this properly creates trust and legal risk. Mitigation: We design a simple 1-page consent form. Staff to collect from beauty clinic patients who are willing. Zero content published without signed consent.

MED

Cross-Lead Activation (Phase 2) Needs Explicit Approval

Using lead databases from Jayakrishnan's other businesses for WhatsApp or Meta targeting requires his clear authorization and compliance with Meta's policy. Mitigation: We don't touch this in Month 1. We table it at the Month 1 review meeting as a Phase 2 opportunity with a clear brief on what's needed from his end.

LOW

Seasonal Demand Dip

Panchakarma demand peaks during Karkidaka (monsoon, June–Sept). Off-peak months could see walkin drops. Mitigation: Beauty clinic and postnatal care are year-round services — we shift ad weight to these during Panchakarma off-season. Seasonal calendar built into content plan.

LOW

Google Review Generation Pace

Getting 10+ reviews in Month 1 requires consistent effort from clinic staff — many clinics skip this step. Mitigation: We design a frictionless review QR code system, brief the staff personally, and track weekly. This is non-negotiable for GMB ranking.
